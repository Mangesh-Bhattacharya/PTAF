# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the PTAF
project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy towards other contributors

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, public or private
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Trolling, or deliberately disruptive/inflammatory comments
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Scope specific to a security tooling project

PTAF is a security automation project. Contributors are expected to:

- Never use this repository to coordinate real attacks against systems you
  don't have explicit authorization to test.
- Never submit modules designed to cause real harm rather than a controlled
  simulation (see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the safety bar).
- Report suspected misuse of the project the same way as a conduct issue
  (see below).

## Enforcement responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and are expected to
take appropriate, fair corrective action in response to any behavior they
deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior by opening a confidential report via the
repository's [private vulnerability reporting](../../security/advisories/new)
page, or by contacting the maintainer directly through their GitHub profile.
All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from common open-source community
guidelines, including the spirit of the Contributor Covenant.
